of The Dress

Evening Dress, 1948

The Spanish-born couturier Cristóbal Balenciaga, regarded by his peers as 'the Master', had moved to Paris from Spain in 1937. His clients, admitted to his salon only after a personal introduction, included many cosmopolitan women of different nationalities. Gloria Guinness, who donated this dress to the V&A, was one of Balenciaga's most loyal customers. The style of this dress reflects the exaggerated padded hip shape of the 'New Look' made famous by Christian Dior. However, its sombre black, and pared down design is very typical of Balenciaga, who was inspired by Spanish religious dress and the paintings of clerics by Francisco de Zurbarán.

Design House: House of Balenciaga
Designer: Cristobal Balenciaga
Year: 1948
Medium: Silk organdie
Culture: French
Source: Victoria & Albert Museum
